# The SC-300 Path

There are dozens of security and identity certifications in the market. Most are not worth your time — at least not yet. The four in this curriculum build on each other technically, are recognized by hiring managers in the roles you are targeting, and map directly to work you are already doing in IAM. What follows is a brutally honest breakdown of each one: format, cost, prep time, and exactly when you are ready to sit it.

<HoloPanel label="THE PATH">

**Recommended sequence:**

```
SC-900 (optional warm-up) → SC-300 (the IAM cert) → Security+ SY0-701 (SecOps + DoD 8570) → CISSP (architect/manager track)
```

The official Microsoft and CompTIA prerequisite pages will not tell you this order. Microsoft calls SC-900 "recommended before SC-300" — technically true, strategically optional. (ISC)² lists no formal prerequisites for CISSP at all. Neither statement tells you what actually prepares you.

SC-300 is the highest-ROI cert for someone doing IAM as a day job. Security+ builds the breadth CISSP expects. CISSP is a capstone that requires cross-domain depth you will not have in year one. Certs are signaling — get them for the door, build the reps for everything after.

</HoloPanel>

## SC-900 — Microsoft Security, Compliance, and Identity Fundamentals

<HoloPanel label="SC-900 · Fundamentals · $99 USD (US)">

**Exam code:** SC-900 &nbsp;|&nbsp; **typically 40–60 questions** &nbsp;|&nbsp; **~60 min** &nbsp;|&nbsp; **$99 USD (US list)**  
**Official URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/credentials/certifications/exams/sc-900/  
**Prep time:** 4–8 weeks (1–2 weeks if you already have hands-on Entra ID experience)

Covers security and compliance concepts, Zero Trust framing, Entra ID basics, Defender suite overview, and Purview fundamentals. Breadth-not-depth — this is a familiarity exam, not a practitioner exam.

**Honest take:** SC-900 is a warm-up, not a hiring signal. No recruiter screens for it standalone, and it will not move the needle on your resume alongside SC-300 or Security+. Take it if you have zero Microsoft cloud cred and want a confidence-building milestone. If you already have hands-on Entra ID time from your current role, skip it and go straight to SC-300.

**You are ready when:**
- You can explain the difference between authentication and authorization without referencing Google
- You know what Entra ID is and have logged into the portal at least once
- You can define Zero Trust in one sentence
- You are scoring above 75% on Microsoft's free practice assessment for SC-900 (linked from the exam's Microsoft Learn page)

</HoloPanel>

## SC-300 — Microsoft Identity and Access Administrator

<HoloPanel label="SC-300 · Associate · $165 USD (US)">

**Exam code:** SC-300 &nbsp;|&nbsp; **typically 40–60 questions** &nbsp;|&nbsp; **~100 min** &nbsp;|&nbsp; **$165 USD (US list)**  
**Official URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/credentials/certifications/exams/sc-300/  
**Prep time:** 3–6 months for an active IAM practitioner

**Skills measured (functional groups):**

| Functional group | Relative weight |
|---|---|
| Implement and manage user identities, groups, and external identities | ~20–25% |
| Implement authentication and access management | ~25–30% |
| Implement access management for apps | ~10–15% |
| Plan and implement identity governance in Entra | ~25–30% |

Microsoft publishes the exact percentage ranges on the live skills-outline PDF and revises them periodically — pull the current "Study guide for Exam SC-300" before you schedule and treat the ranges above as the shape of the exam, not exact figures. The pattern that holds across revisions: governance and authentication/access management together carry the majority of the weight.

Identity governance is where most people underestimate the depth. Entitlement Management (access packages, catalogs, connected organizations), Access Reviews, PIM, and lifecycle workflows: build labs. Do not just read.

**Honest take:** This is the cert. SC-300 maps directly to Entra ID configuration, Conditional Access, PIM, and identity governance — the exact controls you touch on the job. Highest ROI in this stack for your specific role.

**You are ready when:**
- You have configured at least one Conditional Access policy from scratch and set up PIM for a role
- You can explain the difference between a service principal and a managed identity, and when you would use each
- You understand Entitlement Management at the level of access packages, catalogs, and connected organizations
- You are scoring 80%+ consistently on a fresh SC-300 practice exam (not a memorized run)
- The identity governance functional group does not feel like a foreign language

</HoloPanel>

## CompTIA Security+ SY0-701

<HoloPanel label="Security+ SY0-701 · Foundational/Intermediate · $404 USD">

**Exam code:** SY0-701 (current version; SY0-601 retired July 2024) &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Max 90 questions** &nbsp;|&nbsp; **90 min** &nbsp;|&nbsp; **$404 USD list** (vouchers and bundles often run lower)  
**Official URL:** https://www.comptia.org/certifications/security  
**Prep time:** 3–6 months if SC-300 is already in hand

**Exam domain breakdown:**

| Domain | Weight |
|---|---|
| General Security Concepts | 12% |
| Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Mitigations | 22% |
| Security Architecture | 18% |
| Security Operations | 28% |
| Security Program Management & Oversight | 20% |

Security+ is broader and less IAM-specific than SC-300. It covers threat actor types, vulnerability management, incident response, PKI, cryptography fundamentals, and governance/risk/compliance — everything CISSP assumes you already know.

**Honest take:** Worth it for two reasons. First, DoD 8570 / DoD 8140 — if you touch DoD-adjacent work through the Guard, Security+ is the baseline credential for IAM-level positions. Second, it forces the broad SecOps vocabulary that CISSP expects. The 28% Security Operations domain is the hardest portion for pure IAM practitioners. Lean into it.

**You are ready when:**
- SC-300 is credentialed, not in progress
- You can describe incident response phases without a slide deck
- You understand PKI trust chains and certificate lifecycle at a working level
- You are scoring 80%+ on fresh practice exams; the performance-based simulations are not surprising you

</HoloPanel>

## (ISC)² CISSP

<HoloPanel label="CISSP · Senior/Manager Level · $749 USD">

**Exam:** Computerized Adaptive Testing (CAT) &nbsp;|&nbsp; **adaptive question count and time limit** &nbsp;|&nbsp; **$749 USD**  
**Official URL:** https://www.isc2.org/certifications/cissp  
**Prep time:** 12–24 months after SC-300 and Security+ are in hand  
**Experience requirement:** 5 years cumulative paid experience in two or more of the 8 CBK domains. Without it, you become an Associate of ISC2 — you cannot claim the full CISSP until the experience is documented and endorsed. Confirm the current CAT length and question range on the ISC2 exam outline before you book, as ISC2 has revised CAT parameters in the past.

Covers 8 CBK domains: Security and Risk Management, Asset Security, Security Architecture, Network Security, **Identity and Access Management (Domain 5)**, Security Assessment, Security Operations, and Software Development Security. Domain 5 is your strongest head start. The CAT format probes for weaknesses — breadth across all eight is not optional.

**Honest take:** Career-defining for the management and architect track. Do not sit it until SC-300 and Security+ are both credentialed and you have at least 3–4 years of documented IAM experience. CISSP done right is a capstone — done wrong, it is an expensive reschedule.

**You are ready when:**
- SC-300 and Security+ are both on your wall, not in progress
- You have documented 4–5 years of professional security/IAM work across two or more CBK domains
- You can give a 5-minute summary of any of the 8 domains, including the ones outside your daily work
- You are scoring 80%+ on adaptive-format CISSP practice exams
- You have an endorser lined up — (ISC)² requires endorsement from an existing CISSP member to finalize the credential

</HoloPanel>

<WarStory title="The CISSP Fast-Track Lesson">
A colleague at a regional bank — seven years in IT, strong network engineering background — decided in year four that CISSP was the move. No prior security certs. Crammed eight months, passed on the first attempt. Genuinely impressive.

Then he started interviewing for architect roles. Job descriptions asked for Entra ID, Conditional Access, PIM — the SC-300 stack. His CISSP showed breadth; interview panels found the gap. He spent 18 months backfilling SC-300 and building Entra ID lab time he would have built anyway in the right sequence.

The IAM engineer job went to someone with SC-300 and two years of Entra ID hands-on. The CISSP job went to someone with ten years of broad security experience across four CBK domains. The sequence matters because the experience you accumulate on the way to each cert is the substance behind the signal.
</WarStory>

<ProTip>
The right cert order is not the official prerequisite list. Microsoft says SC-900 is "recommended" before SC-300 — meaning it is optional. (ISC)² lists no formal prereqs for CISSP at all. Take those pages as minimum bars, not optimal sequencing. If IAM is your day job, SC-300 is your first cert. Everything else sequences from there.
</ProTip>
