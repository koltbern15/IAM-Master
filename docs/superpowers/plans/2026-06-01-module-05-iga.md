### 01-iga-fundamentals — "IGA Fundamentals: The Access Lifecycle" — ~2,900 words

**Topics / study material:**
- **What IGA is.** `<Definition term="Identity Governance and Administration (IGA)">` — the discipline (and tool category) that answers "who has access to what, why, and should they still?" by combining **identity administration** (provisioning/deprovisioning, fulfilment) with **identity governance** (policy, access certification, segregation of duties, analytics). Gartner coined IGA by merging the earlier IAG (governance) and user-administration/provisioning markets. Cite the Gartner Magic Quadrant for Identity Governance and Administration definition `[VERIFY current MQ year/wording]`.
- **The access lifecycle (the spine of the section).** Request → Approval → Provisioning (fulfilment) → Certification (recertification/attestation) → Revocation, governed continuously by policy and SoD. Teach each stage as a primitive:
  - **Access request** — self-service request portal; requestable items modeled as roles/entitlements/access profiles. Contrast cart-style request UX vs birthright (automatic) grants.
  - **Approval** — single- or multi-stage workflow; approver = manager, resource owner, or role owner; escalation/SLA. `<Definition term="approval workflow">`.
  - **Provisioning / fulfilment** — automated (connector writes the entitlement) vs manual (work item / ticket to a fulfiller). Tie to **SCIM 2.0** (RFC 7643 schema, RFC 7644 protocol) as the standard provisioning protocol — cross-link Module 2 `02-protocols/06-scim`.
  - **Certification / recertification** — periodic attestation that access is still appropriate; reviewer affirms or revokes. `<Definition term="access certification campaign">`.
  - **Revocation / deprovisioning** — closing the loop; orphaned/rogue access detection.
- **Joiner–Mover–Leaver (JML).** `<Definition term="Joiner-Mover-Leaver (JML)">` lifecycle. **Joiner** — birthright access on hire (RBAC-driven). **Mover** — access recalculation on department/manager/job change; the classic failure is **access accumulation / privilege creep** where movers keep old access. **Leaver** — timely deprovisioning on termination; the regulated risk is **orphaned accounts**. Authoritative anchors: NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 control families **AC (Access Control)** — AC-2 Account Management, AC-5 Separation of Duties, AC-6 Least Privilege — and **AU**/IA where relevant. Cite NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5.
- **RBAC vs ABAC.** `<Definition term="Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)">` — access via role assignment; standardized in **ANSI INCITS 359-2012 (RBAC)**, originating in the NIST/Ferraiolo-Kuhn RBAC model (cite NIST RBAC model). `<Definition term="Attribute-Based Access Control (ABAC)">` — access from policy evaluating subject/resource/environment attributes; cite **NIST SP 800-162** (Guide to ABAC Definition and Considerations). Teach the trade-off: RBAC = predictable, auditable, but suffers role explosion; ABAC = flexible/dynamic, but harder to audit and reason about. Note the common hybrid (RBAC for coarse birthright + ABAC for fine-grained context).
- **Recertification campaigns.** Types: user access reviews (manager certifies their reports), application/entitlement owner reviews, role membership reviews, privileged-access reviews. Cadence driven by risk + compliance (e.g., SOX quarterly for financially-significant apps). Pitfalls: **rubber-stamping** (reviewers bulk-approve without scrutiny) and reviewer fatigue. Tie to compliance drivers: **SOX 404 ITGC** access reviews, **PCI-DSS 4.0 Requirement 7** (need-to-know least privilege) and **Requirement 8** (identity management) — cite PCI-DSS v4.0 `[VERIFY exact req sub-numbers]`.
- **Where IGA sits vs IdP vs PAM.** IGA governs *who should have what* across all systems (the system of record for access policy); the IdP (Entra/Okta) authenticates; PAM (Module 4) vaults/brokers privileged sessions. IGA often feeds birthright groups the IdP consumes.

### 06-sod-and-rbac-design — "SoD Modeling & RBAC Design" — ~2,800 words

**Topics / study material:**
- **Segregation/Separation of Duties (SoD).** `<Definition term="Segregation of Duties (SoD)">` — no single individual should control all phases of a sensitive process (e.g., create vendor AND approve payment). The control objective behind SOX ITGC. Anchor in **NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 AC-5 (Separation of Duties)** and the classic **toxic combination** concept. Cite NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5.
- **Modeling SoD.** Define **SoD policy / ruleset** as pairs (or sets) of conflicting functions/entitlements; **preventive** enforcement (block the request that would create a conflict) vs **detective** enforcement (certification flags the existing conflict) vs **compensating controls / mitigating controls** (allow with logging + extra review when the business must). Granularity matters: function-level (business activity) vs raw-entitlement-level rulesets. Reference **SAP GRC Access Control** as the canonical heavy-duty SoD ruleset engine and how IGA platforms (SailPoint, Saviynt) integrate or replicate it `[VERIFY]`. Tie to financial-services examples (banking maker/checker).
- **Role engineering & role mining.** `<Definition term="role mining">` — bottom-up discovery of candidate roles by clustering users who hold the same entitlements (data-driven), vs **top-down role engineering** (business-process-driven role design). Most programs do both ("hybrid role mining"). Cite NIST RBAC / SailPoint/Saviynt role-mining docs `[VERIFY]`.
- **Role explosion / role bloat (the central pitfall).** `<Definition term="role explosion">` — when fine-grained, per-exception roles proliferate until there are more roles than users, destroying the auditability RBAC was meant to provide. Causes: over-specific roles, one-role-per-app sprawl, never retiring roles. Mitigations: two-tier model (broad **business roles** + narrow **IT/technical roles**, per SailPoint vocabulary from section 02), birthright vs requestable separation, attribute-driven assignment (ABAC) for the dynamic dimension instead of minting a role per attribute combo, and periodic **role certification/cleanup**.
- **RBAC vs ABAC in design (build on section 01).** When to use a role vs a policy: stable, org-aligned, auditable access → role; context-dependent, high-cardinality, dynamic → attribute policy. Cite NIST SP 800-162 (ABAC) again. Discuss the **PBAC / policy-based** convergence narrative briefly `[VERIFY framing]`.
- **A practical design method.** 1) inventory entitlements; 2) mine + interview for candidate roles; 3) define a two-tier role model; 4) write the SoD ruleset with the business; 5) wire preventive checks into the request flow + detective checks into certifications; 6) govern roles themselves (role owners, role certification) to prevent drift/explosion.

### 03-okta (8)
1. What is Okta Universal Directory? → A flexible cloud profile store that aggregates user attributes from many sources (HR, AD/LDAP, apps) into one Okta profile, with profile mappings transforming attributes between apps and the directory — the foundation for attribute-driven assignment.
2. What does Okta Lifecycle Management automate? → JML: inbound/HR-as-master provisioning, group-rule-driven membership, app assignment, and deprovisioning on deactivation — provisioning to apps via SCIM and the Okta Integration Network.
3. What is an Okta group rule? → A rule that dynamically adds/removes users from a group based on an attribute expression, driving birthright access and app assignment without manual membership management.
4. What is Okta Workflows? → Okta's no-code/low-code automation engine (an if-this-then-that flow builder) for custom identity automation — bespoke JML logic, ticketing, notifications, and provisioning beyond out-of-box connectors.
5. What is Okta Identity Governance (OIG)? → Okta's IGA SKU adding Access Requests (self-service + approvals, Slack/Teams-native), Access Certifications, and Entitlement Management on top of Universal Directory and Lifecycle Management. [VERIFY SKU bundling]
6. What does Okta API Access Management provide? → Okta acting as an OAuth 2.0 authorization server — custom authorization servers issuing scoped access tokens for your own APIs, with scopes, claims, and access policies. Governance of machine/API authorization.
7. When is Okta a better IGA fit than SailPoint? → When the IdP is already Okta and the estate is SaaS/SCIM-friendly — fast deployment and tight IdP integration. SailPoint pulls ahead for deep, customizable role/SoD modeling in complex on-prem-heavy enterprises. [VERIFY OIG SoD depth]
8. How does Okta provision access to downstream SaaS apps? → Via SCIM (RFC 7643/7644) and pre-built integrations in the Okta Integration Network, driven by app assignment in Lifecycle Management.

### 04-saviynt-oneidentity-omada (8)
1. What is Saviynt EIC and what makes it distinctive? → Enterprise Identity Cloud — a cloud-native platform converging IGA, CIEM, application access governance, and cloud PAM, with strong fine-grained entitlement governance (SAP/cloud) and a usage/peer analytics layer. [VERIFY module names]

6. What does "converged platform" mean in Saviynt's pitch? → Delivering IGA together with cloud entitlement (CIEM) and application/cloud security in one platform, rather than stitching separate IGA + cloud-security tools.

8. Name three IGA platforms outside the two market leaders that a banking RFP might include. → Saviynt (EIC), One Identity (Identity Manager), and Omada (Identity / Identity Cloud).

### 05-entra-id-governance (6)
1. What license tier gates Microsoft Entra ID Governance features like access packages and access reviews? → Entra ID P2 / the Entra ID Governance add-on (bundled in Entra Suite). [VERIFY exact gating]
2. What is an access package in Entra entitlement management? → A bundle of resources (groups, apps, SharePoint sites) plus assignment policies defining who can request it, the approval flow, and expiration — the requestable unit of entitlement management.
3. What is a catalog in Entra entitlement management? → A container that groups related resources and access packages and lets catalog owners delegate governance to resource owners — the boundary for who can manage and include resources.
4. What can an Entra access review attest? → Group membership, app assignments, PIM role eligibility/assignments, and B2B guest access — reviewed by self, manager, group owner, or selected reviewers, with auto-apply and inactivity-based recommendations.
5. What do Entra Lifecycle Workflows automate, and what attributes trigger them? → Automated JML tasks (e.g., generate a Temporary Access Pass, add to groups, email the manager, disable account, remove licenses) triggered on Joiner/Mover/Leaver by attributes like employeeHireDate and employeeLeaveDateTime. 6. How does Entra entitlement management govern external (B2B guest) access? → Via connected organizations and access packages that guests request — with approval, automatic expiration, and removal — so guest access self-expires instead of lingering.

### 06-sod-and-rbac-design (8)
1. What is Segregation of Duties (SoD)? → The control principle that no single person should control all phases of a sensitive process (e.g., create a vendor AND approve its payment). Anchored in NIST SP 800-53 AC-5 and the foundation of SOX ITGC.
2. What is a toxic combination? → A set of entitlements/functions that, held together by one identity, create an unacceptable SoD conflict (e.g., the pair that lets one person both initiate and approve a payment).
3. Preventive vs detective SoD enforcement? → Preventive blocks the request that would create a conflict before access is granted; detective flags the existing conflict during a certification or scan after the fact. Mitigating/compensating controls allow a needed conflict with extra monitoring/review.
4. Top-down role engineering vs bottom-up role mining? → Top-down designs roles from business processes/job functions; bottom-up (role mining) discovers candidate roles by clustering users with similar entitlements from actual data. Mature programs blend both.
5. What is role explosion and what causes it? → The proliferation of fine-grained roles until there are nearly as many (or more) roles than users, destroying RBAC's auditability. Caused by over-specific roles, one-role-per-app sprawl, and never retiring roles.
6. Name two mitigations for role explosion. → A two-tier model (broad business roles + narrow IT/technical roles) and using attribute-based assignment (ABAC) for high-cardinality/dynamic access instead of minting a role per attribute combination; plus periodic role certification/cleanup.
7. When should you model access as a role vs an attribute policy? → Role for stable, org-aligned, auditable access; attribute (ABAC) policy for context-dependent, dynamic, high-cardinality access. Minting a role for every dynamic condition feeds role explosion.
8. What is SAP GRC Access Control's relevance to SoD? → It is the canonical heavy-duty SoD ruleset engine for SAP environments; IGA platforms (SailPoint, Saviynt) integrate with or replicate its conflict rulesets for cross-system SoD. [VERIFY]
